German Spaetzle Dumplings

by Jeffrey Sanford

These are traditional German dumplings that are super tasty. They are different than the dumplings we see in American or Chinese cooking. They make a wonder quick side-dish to a German meal or anytime. For an extra tasty treeat, skip the butter and use few pieces of bcon and the bacon grease that it produces! Mahlzeit!

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch freshly ground white pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 gallon hot water
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
Directions

Mix together flour, salt, white pepper, and nutmeg. Beat eggs well, and add alternately with the milk to the dry ingredients. Mix until smooth.

Press dough through spaetzle maker, or a large holed sieve or metal grater.

Drop a few at a time into simmering liquid. Cook 5 to 8 minutes. Drain well.

Saute cooked spaetzle in butter or margarine. Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley on top, and serve.
